What advice would you give to a person aspiring a job in tech?

Your Friendly Neighborhood Dev - June 14

  • Start your own little projects when you can, so you can give yourself the opportunity to put what you’ve learned into practice somewhere where you’ll see immediate results (no waiting on co-workers to approve Code Reviews, no needing enough permissions to make changes, etc), and figuring out what works well and what doesn’t. You might even end up with something cool that could actually make you some money
  • Be a life long learner. In tech there is so much to know from frontend to microservices to design to product development. Sure, you won't be able to learn and master everything. Rather focus on what youare passionate about and the progress you have made so far
  • Always try to remix the result if you followed a tutorial/walktrhough. See if you can implement the thing in another way, add a new feature, try explaining it to someone else
  • Put yourself out there. Networking sounds lame, but going to meetups, talking to people involved in the tech community or attending hackathons opens up many opportunities you would find on regular job listings
